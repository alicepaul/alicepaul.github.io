Workshop: Intro to R for Health Data Science

Workshop Dates: August 22nd to 24th, 9 am to 12 pm

R is a free and powerful programming language designed for statistical analysis. This 3-day workshop will introduce participants to the basics of R and RStudio, data transformation and visualization, and regression analysis in R. This course is designed for students, staff, and faculty in health data science who want to improve their programming skills and be able to analyze their own data. No prior experience with R or any other programming language is required. At the end of the workshop, participants will leave with a resource to expand on the skills and tools learned.

The instructor for this workshop is Dr. Alice Paul, an Assistant Professor of Biostatistics at Brown University with a background in algorithms, optimization, and data science. She teaches several data science and computational courses, including PHP 1560/2560: Statistical Programming in R. Please email Dr. Paul (alice_paul@brown.edu) with any questions.
